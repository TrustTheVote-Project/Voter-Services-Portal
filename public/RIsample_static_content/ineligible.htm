To register to vote in Virginia, you must:

  • Be a United States citizen
  • Be a resident of Virginia
  • Be at least 18 years old by the next general election
  • Have had your voting rights restored if you have ever been convicted of a felony
  • Have had your capacity restored if you have ever been declared mentally incapacitated in a Circuit Court

Based on your answers to our questions about eligibility as a voter, you may not be eligible to vote in Virginia. If you have any questions you should contact your local voter registration office or the Department of Elections. If you still feel that you are in fact eligible to vote print the voter registration form, sign it, and mail or deliver it to your local voter registration office.

Please be aware that making a false statement on a voter registration application is punishable under Virginia law as a felony. Violators may be sentenced up to 10 years in prison, or up to 12 months in jail and/or fined up to $2,500.
