Your voter record indicates that you have registered to vote as an overseas or military voter. If you are a legal Virginia resident, and need to change your voter registration, you may use the Federal Post Card Application (FPCA) to register to vote and apply for your absentee ballot at the same time.

The Federal Voting Assistance Program (FVAP) web site provides a Virginia Voting Assistance Guide (.pdf) explaining how to complete your FPCA and a link to start the application process. By submitting a completed FPCA in Virginia, you will be sent ballots for all Federal elections in which you are eligible to vote through the end of the next calendar year.

You may also register to vote or change your registration information and request absentee ballots by filling out and submitting the Virginia voter registration application and absentee ballot application. However, using the FPCA is recommended for the convenience of a single form allowing electronic submission of registration by fax or scanned email attachment.

For more details, see our web page Military and Overseas Registrations.
